# Noun
- Determiner (Countable/Uncountable) + Adjective + Singular/Plural
    - the と all では、all the dogs の語順となる

## Countable/Uncountable

### basic
1. 具体的で決まった形があるか否か
2. 状態
    - Look at the size of **those pumpkins.**
    - **How much pumpkin** did you put in the soup?

### Uncountable の数え方
- **a glass of** wine/water/milk
- **a cup of** coffee/tea
- **a bowl of** soup
- **a slice of** bread
- **a sheet of** paper
- **a piece of** cheese/cake/information/news/luggage/baggage/fruit/furniture
- **a spoonful of** sugar
- **a gallon of** gasoline/water
- **a pound of** sugar/tea/butter

### 単数形でも複数だと感じられる、複数形でも単数だと感じられるケース
- 集団は単数扱い
    - **My family** are/is all into skiing.
        - He comes from **a large family**
        - There are **2 Indian families** living near us.
    - are が使われていることに注意
    - だが、is を使えばよい
        - 最近のアメリカ英語では個々の構成メンバーが意識されていても一貫して単数と扱う強い傾向がある
- The + Adj は複数扱い
    - The rich **are** sometimes stingy.
- まとめて考える場合は単数扱い
    - **20 cigarettes** is a lot to smoke in a day, don't you think?
    - **20 miles** is a long way to run.
- 2 つ以上のものが絡む動作は複数
    - Did you get a direct flight or did you have to **change planes?**
    - Some people bow while others **shake hands.**
    - I **made friends** with Karen.
