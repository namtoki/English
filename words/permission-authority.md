# Permission and Authority

This category contains words related to granting permission, authorization, and official approval.

## Words

### Noun
- `permission` /pəˈmɪʃən/           ([Neutral] 許可)                You need permission to enter this building.
- `approval` /əˈpruːvəl/            ([Formal] 承認、賛成)           The plan received unanimous approval.
- `disapproval` /ˌdɪsəˈpruːvəl/     ([Formal] 不承認)               She showed her disapproval by shaking her head.
- `authorization` /ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən/ ([Formal] 認可)                 You need authorization to access these files.
- `authority` /ɔːˈθɒrəti/           ([Neutral] 権威、権限)          He has no authority over me.
- `clout` /klaʊt/                   ([Neutral] 影響力)              She has a lot of clout in the industry.
- `sway` /sweɪ/                     ([Neutral] 影響力)              He has considerable sway over the board.
- `leverage` /ˈlɛvərɪdʒ/            ([Formal] 影響力)               The company used its leverage in negotiations.
- `pull` /pʊl/                      ([Casual] 影響力)               She has a lot of pull with management.
- `heft` /hɛft/                     ([Casual] 影響力)               His opinion carries real heft around here.
- `override` /ˈoʊvərraɪd/           ([Formal] 無効化)               The system has a manual override.

### Verb
- `grant` /ɡrɑːnt/                  ([Formal] 認可する、付与する)   The committee granted my request.
- `allow` /əˈlaʊ/                   ([Neutral] 許可する)            My parents allowed me to go to the party.
- `permit` /pəˈmɪt/                 ([Formal] 許可する)             Smoking is not permitted here.
- `approve` /əˈpruːv/               ([Neutral] 承認する、賛成)      The committee approved the new policy.
- `disapprove` /ˌdɪsəˈpruːv/        ([Formal] 不承認する)           I disapprove of your decision.
- `authorize` /ˈɔːθəraɪz/           ([Formal] 権限を与える)         Only authorized personnel can enter this area.
- `override` /ˌoʊvərˈraɪd/          ([Formal] 無効にする)           The president can override the veto.
- `supersede` /ˌsuːpərˈsiːd/        ([Formal] 取って代わる)         The new law will supersede the old one.
- `overrule` /ˌoʊvərˈruːl/          ([Formal] 却下する)             The judge overruled the objection.
- `nullify` /ˈnʌlɪˌfaɪ/             ([Formal] 無効化する)           The court nullified the contract.
- `veto` /ˈviːtoʊ/                  ([Formal] 拒否権行使)           The governor vetoed the bill.
- `annul` /əˈnʌl/                   ([Formal] 無効にする)           They decided to annul the marriage.
- `revoke` /rɪˈvoʊk/                ([Formal] 取り消す)             His license was revoked.
- `repeal` /rɪˈpiːl/                ([Formal] 廃止する)             Congress repealed the law.
- `rescind` /rɪˈsɪnd/               ([Formal] 撤回する)             The company rescinded the offer.
- `invalidate` /ɪnˈvælɪˌdeɪt/       ([Formal] 無効にする)           This will invalidate your warranty.

### Adjective
- `unauthorized` /ʌnˈɔːθəraɪzd/     ([Formal] 権限のない)           This is an unauthorized copy.
- `authoritarian` /ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriən/ ([Writing] 権威主義の)          The country is run by an authoritarian regime.
- `authoritative` /ɔːˈθɒrɪtətɪv/    ([Writing] 権威のある)          This is an authoritative biography.
- `overriding` /ˌoʊvərˈraɪdɪŋ/      ([Formal] 最重要の)             Safety is the overriding concern.
