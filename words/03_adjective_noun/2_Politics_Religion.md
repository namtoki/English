# Politics and Religion

## Political Ideologies
> `democracy` /dɪˈmɑːkrəsi/         ([Neutral] 民主主義)            Democracy is the foundation of our government.
  - `democratic` /ˌdeməˈkrætɪk/     ([Neutral] 民主的な 民主主義の) They live in a democratic society.
> `capitalism` /ˈkæpɪtəlɪzəm/       ([Neutral] 資本主義)            Capitalism encourages free markets.
  - `capitalist` /ˈkæpɪtəlɪst/      ([Neutral] 資本主義者、資本家)  He's a successful capitalist.
  - `capitalistic` /ˌkæpɪtəˈlɪstɪk/ ([Formal] 資本主義的な)         A capitalistic economy.

  - `socialistic` /ˌsoʊʃəˈlɪstɪk/   ([Formal] 社会主義的な)         Socialistic policies.
> `conservatism` /kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/  ([Formal] 保守主義)             Political conservatism values tradition.
  - `conservative` /kənˈsɜːrvətɪv/  ([Neutral] 保守的な、保守派)    He has conservative views.
> `liberalism` /ˈlɪbərəlɪzəm/       ([Writing] 自由主義)            Political liberalism emphasizes individual rights.
  - `liberal` /ˈlɪbərəl/            ([Neutral] 自由主義的な 進歩的) She has liberal political views.
  - `liberalization`                ([Writing] 自由化)              Economic liberalization boosted growth.
  - `liberalize` /ˈlɪbərəlaɪz/      ([Writing] 自由化する)          The government liberalized trade policies.
> `nationalism` /ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/      ([Neutral] 国家主義、民族主義)  Rising nationalism affected policy.
  - `nationalist` /ˈnæʃənəlɪst/     ([Neutral] 国家主義者)          He's a strong nationalist.
  - `nationalistic`                 ([Neutral] 国家主義的な)        Nationalistic sentiments grew.
> `communism` /ˈkɑːmjənɪzəm/        ([Neutral] 共産主義)            Communism advocates class equality.
  - `communist` /ˈkɑːmjənɪst/       ([Neutral] 共産主義者)          The communist party won seats.
> `populism` /ˈpɑːpjəlɪzəm/         ([Neutral] 大衆主義)            Populism appeals to ordinary people.
  - `populist` /ˈpɑːpjəlɪst/        ([Neutral] 大衆主義者)          He's a populist politician.
> `authoritarianism`                ([Formal] 権威主義)             The country fell into authoritarianism.
  - `authoritarian`                 ([Formal] 権威主義的な)         An authoritarian regime.
> `totalitarianism`                 ([Formal] 全体主義)             Totalitarianism controls all aspects of life.
  - `totalitarian`                  ([Formal] 全体主義的な)         A totalitarian state.
> `fascism` /ˈfæʃɪzəm/              ([Formal] ファシズム、全体主義) Fascism rose in the 1930s.
  - `fascist` /ˈfæʃɪst/             ([Formal] ファシスト)           The fascist government fell.
> `anarchy` /ˈænərki/               ([Formal] 無政府状態、無秩序)   The country descended into anarchy.
  - `anarchic` /əˈnɑːrkɪk/          ([Formal] 無政府主義的な)       The situation became anarchic.
