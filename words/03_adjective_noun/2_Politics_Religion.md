## Religion
> `monastery` /ˈmɑːnəsteri/         ([Neutral] 修道院)              The monastery was home to 20 monks.
  - `convent` /ˈkɑːnvent/           ([Neutral] 修道院、女子修道院)  She joined a convent at age 18.
  - `abbey` /ˈæbi/                  ([Neutral] 修道院、大修道院)    The abbey was built in the 12th century.
  - `cloister` /ˈklɔɪstər/          ([Formal] 修道院、回廊)         Monks walked silently through the cloister.
  - `priory` /ˈpraɪəri/             ([Formal] 小修道院)             The priory housed a small community.
  - `friary` /ˈfraɪəri/             ([Formal] 托鉢修道会の修道院)   The Franciscan friary welcomed visitors.
  - `nunnery` /ˈnʌnəri/             ([Old-fashioned] 尼僧院)        The nunnery was established in 1400.

## Government Systems & Authority
- `sovereign` /ˈsɑːvrɪn/            ([Formal] 主権を持つ、独立した) A sovereign nation has full authority.
- `autonomous` /ɔːˈtɑːnəməs/        ([Formal] 自治的な、自律的な)  The region is autonomous.
- `abdication` /ˌæbdɪˈkeɪʃən/       ([Formal] 退位)                 His abdication shocked the nation.

## Political Ideologies
- `liberalism` /ˈlɪbərəlɪzəm/     ([Writing] 自由主義)            Political liberalism emphasizes individual rights.
- `liberalization` /ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ ([Writing] 自由化)         Economic liberalization boosted growth.
- `liberalize` /ˈlɪbərəlaɪz/      ([Writing] 自由化する)          The government liberalized trade policies.
- `anarchy` /ˈænərki/               ([Formal] 無政府状態、無秩序)   The country descended into anarchy.
- `anarchic` /əˈnɑːrkɪk/          ([Formal] 無政府主義的な)       The situation became anarchic.
- `anarchist` /ˈænərkɪst/         ([Formal] 無政府主義者)         The anarchist was arrested.
